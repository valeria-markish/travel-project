WHY TOKYO?

Tokyo, between tradition and ultramodern

Tokyo is a city that inspires the senses. Whether from its more futuristic side, full of neon, technological attractions and streets that look like an urban fashion parade, or from its more traditional side, with its gardens, palaces and vestiges of the Edo period, for example. Everything about it is superlative.

Tokyo is famous for its fashion and shopping areas, which include the fashionable shopping districts of Harajuku and Shibuya, as well as the luxury flagship stores of Ginza.

Shinjuku is a thriving commercial district, combining gleaming skyscrapers and lively alleyways filled with small bars. Asakusa is the heart of central Tokyo, with ancient temples and traditional shops.

Tokyo is a foodie paradise with more Michelin-starred restaurants than any other city in the world, along with hundreds of affordable and delicious ramen shops.

CAFES

My favourite cafes in Tokyo

Honey Toast Cafe Akihabara

Honey Toast Cafe is a community cafe where you can enjoy a "special version" of Pasela's famous honey toast topped with fluffy handmade bread. In a spacious space with sunlight shining all over the window, they serve their signature homemade and freshly baked honey toast. Enjoy it with plenty of honey!

Address:

1-1-10 Sotokanda 1F, Chiyoda 101-0021 Prefectura de Tokio

What I like about it

The variety of ways you can eat the honey toast! It's a must cafe for people who enjoy desserts!

NINJA Cafe & Bar

Ninja Experience Cafe Harajuku in Tokyo, Japan, are hands-on entertainment cafe based on the concept of "Ninja" / "Shinobi", that is! Not only can you enjoy the experience of ninja training by dressing up in ninja costume, but they also offer special ninja dishes and desserts using ninja techniques (Ninjutsu) as a restaurant.

Address:

3-27-14 Nishiasakusa 1F, Taito 111-0035 Prefectura de Tokio

What I like about it

The food, the costumes, the experience... everything!

Takizme

The tea time provided by Takizume is not just a time to enjoy tea and sweets. They offer two types of new tea experiences that incorporate meditation to enjoy with all five senses, as well as a tea salon where you can enjoy a break from your daily routine.

Address:

1-28-7 Higashishinagawa, Shinagawa 140-0002 Prefectura de Tokio

What I like about it

The best place to enjoy the traditional ceremony of tea in Tokyo and also to enjoy all the traditional dishes and desserts that go with the tea!
